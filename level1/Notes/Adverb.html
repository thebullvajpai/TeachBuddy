1. What is an Adverb?

An adverb is a word that adds more information about a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.

In simple words:
üëâ An adverb tells us how, when, where, how often, or to what extent something happens.

I often read books. (how often)
She is very happy. (extent)

2. Why are Adverbs Important?

  • They make actions clear and detailed.
  • They help describe time, place, manner, frequency, and degree.

‚úÖ She speaks clearly.

3. What Can an Adverb Modify?

3.1 Adverb Modifies a Verb

He works hard.
She sings beautifully.

3.2 Adverb Modifies an Adjective

She is very intelligent.
The movie was extremely interesting.

3.3 Adverb Modifies Another Adverb

He ran very fast.
She spoke quite softly.

4. Types of Adverbs (Very Important)

4.1 Adverb of Manner

Tells us how an action is done.

He drives carefully.
She answered politely.

4.2 Adverb of Time

I will call you tomorrow.
She arrived late.

4.3 Adverb of Place

Come here.
The children are playing outside.

4.4 Adverb of Frequency

I always wake up early.
He never lies.

4.5 Adverb of Degree

She is very tired.
The bag is too heavy.

5. Position of Adverbs in a Sentence

Yesterday, I met my teacher.
She often studies at night.
He speaks English fluently.

Note: Position depends on the type of adverb.
