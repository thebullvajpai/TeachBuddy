3. Types of Adjectives (Very Important)

3.1 Adjective of Quality

These adjectives tell us what kind of a person or thing.

Examples: good, bad, tall, short, honest, brave

He is a good boy.
She has a kind nature.

3.2 Adjective of Quantity

These adjectives tell us how much (for uncountable nouns).

Examples: some, little, much, enough

I have some water.
There is little milk left.

3.3 Adjective of Number

These adjectives tell us how many or in what order.

Types

  • Cardinal: one, two, three
  • Ordinal: first, second, third
Three students are absent.
He came first in the race.

3.4 Demonstrative Adjectives

Used to point out specific nouns.

Forms: this, that, these, those

This book is mine.
Those shoes are expensive.

Note: When followed by a noun, they act as adjectives.

3.5 Possessive Adjectives

These adjectives show ownership.

Forms: my, your, his, her, its, our, their

My bag is new.
She lost her phone.

3.6 Interrogative Adjectives

Used to ask questions, always followed by a noun.

Forms: which, what, whose

Which book do you want?
Whose pen is this?

3.7 Proper Adjectives

Formed from proper nouns.

  • Indian (from India)
  • American (from America)
  • Shakespearean (from Shakespeare)
I like Indian food.
He studies English literature.

4. Degrees of Comparison

4.1 Positive Degree

Used when no comparison is made.

Ram is tall.

4.2 Comparative Degree

Used to compare two people or things.

Rule: Add -er or use more.

Ram is taller than Shyam.

4.3 Superlative Degree

Used to compare more than two.

Rule: Add -est or use most.

Ram is the tallest boy in the class.
