1. What is a Verb?

A verb is a word that shows:

  • an action
  • a state
  • an occurrence

In simple words:
üëâ A verb tells us what the subject does or what happens.

Examples

  • Action: run, eat, write, speak
  • State: is, am, are, seem, feel
  • Occurrence: become, grow, happen
She runs fast.
I am happy.
He became a doctor.

2. Why are Verbs Important?

  • A sentence is incomplete without a verb.
  • Verbs give life to a sentence.

3. Main Types of Verbs

3.1 Main (Lexical) Verbs

Main verbs show the main action or state.

Examples: eat, write, play, sleep, think

I eat rice daily.
She writes neatly.

3.2 Helping (Auxiliary) Verbs

Helping verbs help the main verb to show tense, mood, or voice.

Common Helping Verbs

  • is, am, are, was, were
  • do, does, did
  • has, have, had
  • will, shall
She is reading a book.
They have finished the work.
I will call you.

  • see ‚Üí saw ‚Üí seen
  • take ‚Üí took ‚Üí taken

6. Transitive and Intransitive Verbs

6.1 Transitive Verbs

A transitive verb needs an object.

Examples: eat (something), read (something)

She reads a book.

6.2 Intransitive Verbs

An intransitive verb does not need an object.

Examples: sleep, cry, laugh

The baby slept.

7. Linking Verbs

Linking verbs connect the subject to more information.

Common Linking Verbs

  • is, am, are, was, were
  • seem, become, feel, look
She is intelligent.
He seems tired.

8. Finite and Non-Finite Verbs

8.1 Finite Verbs

Finite verbs change according to tense and subject.

She writes well.
They write well.

8.2 Non-Finite Verbs

Non-finite verbs do not change with subject or tense.

  • Infinitive: to eat, to play
  • Gerund: eating, playing
  • Participle: eaten, playing
I like to read.
Reading is my hobby.

9. Modal Verbs

Modal verbs show ability, permission, advice, possibility.

Common Modals

  • can, could, may, might
  • should, must, will, would
I can speak English.
You should study daily.
He must follow rules.

10. Verb Tense (Basic Idea)

  • Present: I eat food.
  • Past: I ate food.
  • Future: I will eat food.

(Each tense will be studied separately later.)

13. Practice Tips

  • Identify verbs in every sentence you read
  • Practice verb forms daily
  • Speak using different tenses

14. Final Summary

  • Verbs show action or state
  • They are the heart of sentences
  • Correct verb usage improves fluency
If you master verbs, you can build complete English sentences.
