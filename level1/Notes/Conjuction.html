3. Types of Conjunctions (Very Important)

3.1 Coordinating Conjunctions

These join words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance.

Common Words: and, but, or, nor, for, yet, so

I like tea and coffee.
She is smart but lazy.
You can come early or stay home.
He was tired, so he slept early.

Tip: Remember the acronym FANBOYS ‚Üí For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So

3.2 Subordinating Conjunctions

These join a main clause and a subordinate (dependent) clause.

Common Words: because, although, if, since, unless, while, when, after, before

I stayed home because it was raining.
Although she was tired, she finished her work.
I will call you when I reach home.

6. Practice Tips

  • Connect small sentences using and, but, or
  • Try subordinating words like because, although, when
  • Use correlative pairs in daily sentences
  • Speak sentences aloud to feel the flow

7. Final Summary

  • Conjunctions connect words, phrases, and clauses
  • Coordinating, subordinating, and correlative are main types
  • Correct use improves sentence clarity, logic, and fluency
