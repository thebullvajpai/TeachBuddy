3. Main Types of Tenses

There are three main types:

  1. Present Tense ‚Äì Happening now or regularly
  2. Past Tense ‚Äì Happened before
  3. Future Tense ‚Äì Will happen later

Each tense has four forms:

  • Simple ‚Äì basic action
  • Continuous / Progressive ‚Äì ongoing action
  • Perfect ‚Äì completed action
  • Perfect Continuous ‚Äì ongoing action that started in the past and is still relevant

4. Present Tense

4.1 Present Simple

  • Usage: Regular actions, facts, habits
  • Structure: Subject + base verb (+ s/es for 3rd person singular)
I eat breakfast every day.
She plays football.
Water boils at 100¬∞C.

4.2 Present Continuous

  • Usage: Actions happening now or around now
  • Structure: Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing
I am reading a book.
They are playing football.
She is studying for the exam.

4.3 Present Perfect

  • Usage: Actions completed but related to present
  • Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle
I have eaten lunch.
She has finished her homework.

4.4 Present Perfect Continuous

  • Usage: Actions started in past and still continuing
  • Structure: Subject + have/has + been + verb+ing
I have been reading for 2 hours.
She has been working here since 2019.

5. Past Tense

5.1 Past Simple

  • Usage: Completed actions at a definite time
  • Structure: Subject + past verb
I went to school yesterday.
She played football.

5.2 Past Continuous

  • Usage: Ongoing action at a specific past time
  • Structure: Subject + was/were + verb+ing
I was reading at 8 pm.
They were watching TV.

5.3 Past Perfect

  • Usage: Action completed before another past action
  • Structure: Subject + had + past participle
I had eaten before he arrived.
She had left when I called.

5.4 Past Perfect Continuous

  • Usage: Ongoing action in past before another action
  • Structure: Subject + had + been + verb+ing
I had been studying for 2 hours before she came.
They had been playing football all evening.

6. Future Tense

6.1 Future Simple

  • Usage: Action that will happen in future
  • Structure: Subject + will + base verb
I will eat an apple.
She will come tomorrow.

6.2 Future Continuous

  • Usage: Ongoing action at a specific future time
  • Structure: Subject + will be + verb+ing
I will be reading at 8 pm.
They will be traveling next week.

6.3 Future Perfect

  • Usage: Action will be completed before a specific future time
  • Structure: Subject + will have + past participle
I will have finished my homework by 6 pm.
She will have left before you arrive.

6.4 Future Perfect Continuous

  • Usage: Ongoing action until a specific future time
  • Structure: Subject + will have been + verb+ing
I will have been studying for 3 hours by 6 pm.
They will have been traveling for 10 hours by then.
