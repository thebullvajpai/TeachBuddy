2. Why are Pronouns Important?

  • They make sentences shorter and smoother.
  • They avoid repeating the same noun again and again.
  • They help in natural communication.

3. Types of Pronouns (Very Important)

Pronouns are divided into many types. Each type has a specific role.

3.1 Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns are used for people or things.

(a) Subject Pronouns

Used as the subject of a sentence.

I am learning English.

She is my friend.

They are playing cricket.

(b) Object Pronouns

Used as the object of a verb or preposition.

He helped me.

I like her.

She is talking to them.

3.2 Possessive Pronouns

Possessive pronouns show ownership.

This book is mine.

That bag is hers.

The house is theirs.

Possessive pronouns are never followed by a noun.

‚ùå This is my book mine.
‚úÖ This book is mine.

3.3 Possessive Adjectives (Related to Pronouns)

These come before a noun.

My book is new.

Her phone is lost.

Their house is big.

Difference:

my book ‚ùå mine book

3.4 Reflexive Pronouns

Reflexive pronouns refer back to the subject itself.

Forms

myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves

I hurt myself.

She prepared the food herself.

They enjoyed themselves.

3.5 Demonstrative Pronouns

Used to point out specific things.

Forms

this, that, these, those

This (near, singular)

That (far, singular)

These (near, plural)

Those (far, plural)

This is my pen.

Those are beautiful flowers.

3.6 Interrogative Pronouns

Used to ask questions.

Forms

who, whom, whose, what, which

Who is calling you?

What is your name?

Which book do you want?

3.7 Relative Pronouns

Used to join two sentences and give extra information.

Forms

who, whom, whose, which, that

This is the boy who won the prize.

I like the movie that you suggested.

3.8 Indefinite Pronouns

Refer to people or things in a general way.

Common Words

someone, anyone, everyone, nobody, something, everything

Someone is at the door.

Everyone makes mistakes.

4. Gender and Pronouns

Masculine: he, him

Feminine: she, her

Neutral: it

Common: they (also used for gender-neutral use)
